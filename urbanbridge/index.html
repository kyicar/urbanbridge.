What is a Fast-Growing City?

A fast-growing city is one that experiences rapid expansion in population, economy, infrastructure, and technology. This growth is driven by factors such as migration, investments, geography, and government plans.

Jakarta, Indonesia‚Äôs capital, is a clear example. With over 10 million residents and more than 30 million in its metropolitan area, Jakarta is among the world‚Äôs most densely populated cities. Recent years have seen major developments, including new highways, a subway system, flood control projects, and smart city initiatives.

Economically, Jakarta attracts large domestic and foreign investments in real estate, technology, and infrastructure. However, rapid growth brings challenges such as traffic congestion, air pollution, land shortages, and flooding.

Population growth fuels demand for land, housing, and public services, while rising investments drive infrastructure but can also cause financial risks like real estate bubbles. Efficient planning and money management are essential to ensure sustainable growth.

We are developing a universal indicator framework that city planners, researchers, and organizations can apply to different urban challenges.

This framework is built on three core measurable dimensions:

  • Per Capita Availability ‚Äì how much of a resource or service exists per person.
  • Accessibility ‚Äì how easily residents can access it within a defined time or distance.
  • Coverage Percentage ‚Äì the proportion of total urban area allocated to it.

By applying these three dimensions to any urban issue (X), users can:

  • Measure the current situation with data,
  • Compare it to international standards,
  • Visualize the results,
  • Identify gaps and opportunities for improvement.

Our goal is to make urban indicators universal, comparable, and data-driven, enabling collaborators worldwide to adopt and expand the framework for various urban topics.

We are now inviting collaborators and partners to contribute data, insights, and applications of this framework to build a shared directory of excellence for healthy, sustainable cities.
